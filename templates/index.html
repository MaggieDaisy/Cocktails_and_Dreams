Welcome to our community, if like us you are passionate to create a new coctails, then join and share your dream recipes with us. If you are wondering how we categorizing our drinks :

  • Sours
    Sours should always contain freshly squeezed, tart, tangy citrus juice, which is often paired with simple sugar syrup to sweeten the mix. The choice of main alcohol is free here and depends mostly on preferences. Sours are usually shaken in a cocktail shaker with ice cubes and served straight up. A great example of Sours is all sidecars, margaritas, and cosmopolitans.
  • Fizz or Collins
    Fizzes should always contain anything with bubbles like prosecco or cava and some type of soda, serving in highballs filled with ice cubes. These cocktails are the most refreshing, very simple to make, and light to drink. A great example of fizzes is Champagne-based cocktails, Collinses, mules, and bucks like Aperol Spritz, Gin and tonic, Mojito, or Bellini.
  • Old-Fashioned
    These are old-school short cocktails, often whiskey-based served in lowball filled with ice cubes. Old-Fashioned always contain sweetened liquor zipped up with something bitter. Sweetness can come from sugar, muddled with fruit, or a sweeter liqueur like triple sec or maraschino. The bitter ingredient can be a few dashes of any bottled bitters like Angostura, or a bitter spirit like an amaro. A great example in this group is Old-Fashioned, Sazerac, and Toronto.
  • Three-parters
    This collection of drinks always contain steered together a three-part combo of booze-like spirit and a lower-alcohol ingredient like vermouth or beloved martini, plus a bitter or a syrup. A great example of three-parters is Manhattan, Brooklyn, Negroni, boulevardier, Rob Roy.
